ANTHRO

US export-control directive forces Anthropic to disable Fable 5 and Mythos 5 for all users

  • Anthropic received a US government export-control directive at 5:21 p.m. ET on Friday, June 12, blocking any foreign national, including those inside the US and Anthropic's own foreign-national employees, from using Fable 5 or Mythos 5.The Neuron Daily
  • Because Anthropic says it cannot separate users by nationality in real time, it disabled both models for all customers; the order cited national security without specifics.The Neuron Daily
  • The Wall Street Journal reported that Amazon researchers flagged the underlying issue, a narrow Fable 5 jailbreak, to US officials; Anthropic says the demo found only known minor software flaws other models can also identify.The Neuron Daily
Why it matters

This is described as the first time the US government has blocked an AI model after its public launch. Using export-control authority to gate a model by user nationality creates a compliance problem that forced a full worldwide shutdown.

Concepts to know

Export-control directive
The Neuron Daily

A government order restricting who can access a technology; here it barred foreign nationals from Anthropic's models, which forced a global shutdown because access could not be filtered by nationality.

x402 protocol
The Neuron Daily

A payment protocol that lets software agents autonomously pay for services; Coinbase used it in new MCP infrastructure so agents can trade crypto and settle transactions.

NPU (Neural Processing Unit)
Brain Buzz

A dedicated chip for AI workloads that Microsoft has required for Copilot+ features; its new SDK lets those features run on discrete GPUs instead, widening hardware support.

World foundation model
Brain Buzz

A model trained to simulate and predict the physical world rather than just language; China's Physis-v0.1 is billed as the first general one, useful for robotics and simulation.
