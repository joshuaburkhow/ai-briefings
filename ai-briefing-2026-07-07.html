AI labs currently relying on broad crawling will need to negotiate access or switch to licensed data — compressing a key cost advantage.

Concepts to know

Training Bot vs. Agent Bot
The Neuron Daily

Cloudflare's new taxonomy distinguishes AI training scrapers (bots that collect web data to train models) from AI agents (bots that browse the web on behalf of users). The two look similar at the network level but serve fundamentally different purposes — and now have different default permissions under Cloudflare policy.

Sol / Terra / Luna Tiers
TLDR AI / AI Breakfast

OpenAI's three-tier naming for GPT-5.6: Sol is the frontier-class tier (priced $5/$30 per 1M tokens), Terra matches GPT-5.5 at half the cost, and Luna targets high-volume low-latency workloads. The structure mirrors Anthropic's Opus/Sonnet/Haiku tiering — both labs now sell the same model family at three price/capability points.

Model Distillation Attack
TLDR AI / Everyday AI

A distillation attack is when an actor systematically queries a frontier model at scale to harvest its outputs, then uses those outputs as training data to build a cheaper clone. Anthropic disclosed to Congress that Alibaba ran the largest known instance: 28.8 million Claude interactions across ~25,000 fraudulent accounts, with the goal of fine-tuning Qwen to match Claude's behavior without paying for training data.

Agentic Autonomy Levels
TLDR AI

A framework for classifying how much independent decision-making an AI agent is allowed to exercise before returning control to a human. Low autonomy limits risk and increases reversibility. The current frontier design is a manager agent that delegates to helper agents, verifies their outputs continuously, and only escalates decisions that require human judgment — enabling hundreds or thousands of parallel agents with controlled blast radius.
